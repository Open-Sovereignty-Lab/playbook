Start here. This section orients you before the practical work begins: first the principle that governs every decision in the Playbook, then the three phases of putting it into practice.

## The foundation

[Module 0: Sovereignty in the Digital Age](/playbook/docs/introduction/module-0/) establishes the principle the rest of the Playbook rests on — that tribal sovereignty is inherent, not granted, and that it extends into the digital domain. Read it before the phases: every later decision about AI tools, cloud platforms, procurement, and technical architecture follows from it.

## The three phases

The implementation work is organized into three phases, each answering a guiding question:

- [Phase 1: Pre-Work](/playbook/docs/introduction/phase-1/) — *How do we prepare our tribe, our employees, and our citizens to use these tools responsibly?* Organizational readiness, data classification and sovereignty mapping, policy and legal frameworks, AI literacy, and procurement readiness.
- [Phase 2: Execution](/playbook/docs/introduction/phase-2/) — *How do we leverage and operationalize the available resources and technology?* Vendor evaluation and selection, technical architecture and data protection, contract and data-custody frameworks, FPIC implementation, and pilot design.
- [Phase 3: Monitoring and Evaluation](/playbook/docs/introduction/phase-3/) — *How do we ensure we are seeing the outcomes we want?* Performance and compliance monitoring, values-aligned evaluation, community feedback and governance loops, and red-teaming against tribal values.

> The Phase pages are outlines today — their detailed content is under active development.

Once you are grounded here, the [Guides](/playbook/docs/guides/), [Templates](/playbook/docs/templates/), [Tools](/playbook/docs/tools/), and [Case Studies](/playbook/docs/case-studies/) provide the reusable resources that support each phase.
